Sweet Bonanza vs Sweet Bonanza 1000

Same candy world, different ceilings. Which one should you play?

Pragmatic Play's Sweet Bonanza is one of the most-played slots ever made. In June 2024 they released Sweet Bonanza 1000 — a sequel that keeps everything players love and pushes the win ceiling to a new level. But the two games serve different players, and picking the wrong one for your bankroll and goals is an easy mistake to make.

This is the definitive side-by-side breakdown.

The Same DNA

Before getting into differences, it's worth appreciating how much is shared. Both games run on a 6×5 grid with cluster pays — winning clusters of 8 or more identical symbols. Both use the tumble mechanic where winning symbols are removed and new ones fall in, chaining additional wins. Both trigger Free Spins with 4 or more lollipop scatters, award 10 base free spins, and allow retriggering with 3+ scatters during the bonus for +5 spins.

The symbol set is identical — hearts, candy squares, green hexagons, blue ovals (high pays), then apple, plum, watermelon, grapes, banana (low pays), plus the lollipop scatter. If you've played one, you'll be fully at home in the other within seconds.

The Core Difference: Multiplier Bombs

Everything that separates these two games flows from one number: the maximum multiplier bomb value.

In classic Sweet Bonanza, bombs range from 2× to 100×. A 100× bomb on a $1/spin bet during a big win cluster is impressive — potentially returning hundreds of dollars from a single spin. The max win of 21,175× is a real ceiling that has been hit by players in the wild.

In Sweet Bonanza 1000, bombs range from 2× to 1,000×. That single order-of-magnitude jump changes the character of every Free Spins round. The theoretical best-case scenario — multiple 1,000× bombs stacking on a maximum cluster win — is how the 25,000× ceiling is reached. This is an event of extraordinary rarity (roughly 1 in 71 million spins), but the mid-tier bomb potential (say, three 200× bombs stacking) creates wins that simply aren't possible in the original.

Super Free Spins: SB1000's Exclusive Feature

Sweet Bonanza 1000 introduces Super Free Spins — accessible through the 500× Buy Feature. In Super Free Spins, all multiplier bombs that appear have a minimum value of 20×, removing the lower-tier 2×, 5×, and 10× bombs from the pool entirely.

This is a meaningful structural change. In standard Free Spins (both games), many bombs land at 2× or 5× — technically present but barely impactful. Super Free Spins guarantees every bomb is at least a 20× contributor. Combined with the 1,000× ceiling, the range of bomb outcomes is 20× to 1,000× rather than 2× to 100×.

Bonus Frequency: The Key Trade-Off

This is where casual players most often get surprised by SB1000. The Free Spins bonus triggers 1 in every 450 base spins on average, versus 1 in 275–300 in the original — approximately 60% less frequent.

In a 300-spin session, the original Sweet Bonanza statistically delivers 1–2 bonuses. In the same session, SB1000 is likely to deliver zero. This isn't a flaw — it's the architecture of a higher-ceiling game. The bonus rounds, when they arrive, need to carry more weight. The math works out over millions of spins, but in any given session SB1000 demands more patience.

RTP: SB1000 Wins, Narrowly

SB1000's 96.53% RTP is 0.04 percentage points higher than the original's 96.49%. Over 10,000 spins at $1/spin, that's roughly $4 more in expected return — genuinely negligible. The RTP edge is real but not a meaningful reason to choose one game over the other.

More meaningfully, SB1000 has three certified RTP configurations: 96.53%, 95.52%, and 94.51%. Always check the game info panel at your casino. The classic Sweet Bonanza similarly has alternate RTPs (96.21% with Ante Bet, 94.03% Buy Feature), but both games should run at their headline rate in standard play at reputable operators.

If you love Sweet Bonanza and want to know what happens when the bombs go to 1,000×, SB1000 is exactly that experiment. If you're playing for fun, managing a modest bankroll, or want the most sessions per dollar spent, classic Sweet Bonanza remains the smarter choice.

Comparison FAQs

Is Sweet Bonanza 1000 harder to win than the original?
The bonus triggers less often (1 in 450 vs 1 in 275 spins) and base hits are slightly less frequent (~43% vs ~50%). In a given session, SB1000 will feel more volatile — but both games are officially classified as medium volatility by Pragmatic Play, and SB1000's RTP is marginally higher.
What does the 1000 in Sweet Bonanza 1000 mean?
It refers to the maximum multiplier bomb value of 1,000× that can appear during Free Spins. In the original Sweet Bonanza the maximum bomb is 100×. Multiple bombs can stack before being applied to the total win, enabling the 25,000× max win.
Do both games have Ante Bet and Buy Feature?
Yes. Both games offer Ante Bet (1.25× stake for increased scatter frequency). For Buy Feature, the classic has one option (100× stake); SB1000 has two — a standard buy (100×) and a Super Buy (500×) that guarantees Super Free Spins with minimum 20× bombs.
Which game has better RTP?
Sweet Bonanza 1000 at 96.53% is marginally better than the original's 96.49%. The difference is 0.04 percentage points — effectively negligible in any realistic session length.
