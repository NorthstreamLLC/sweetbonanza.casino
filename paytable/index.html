Sweet Bonanza Paytable

All symbol values, cluster pay rules, and multiplier bomb information.

How Cluster Pays Work

Sweet Bonanza uses a cluster pay system — there are no paylines. Instead, you need 8 or more identical symbols anywhere on the 6×5 grid to form a winning cluster. Larger clusters pay more. Multiple clusters of different symbols can win simultaneously on the same spin.

All pays are expressed as a multiplier of your total stake. A 0.5× pay on a $1 bet returns $0.50.

Scatter Symbol (Lollipop)

Multiplier Bombs (Free Spins Only)

During the Free Spins round, multiplier bombs replace the candy sweet symbols. When bombs land and a tumble sequence ends, all visible multipliers are summed then applied to the entire win. They do not multiply each other — they add.

🟣 50×Rare
💎 100×Very rare

Example: If 3 bombs appear with values 25× + 50× + 100×, they add to a combined 175× multiplier applied to that tumble's win.

Paytable FAQs

What is the highest paying symbol?
The red Heart candy. A 12+ Heart cluster pays 2,500× your stake before any multiplier bombs. During Free Spins, combined with stacked multiplier bombs this is where the game's 21,175× max win potential comes from.
How many symbols for a win?
A minimum of 8 identical symbols anywhere on the grid forms a winning cluster. Symbols don't need to be adjacent in a line — they just need to be the same type.
Is there a wild symbol?
No traditional wild. The lollipop is the scatter. There are no wild substitutions — wins come purely from clusters of matching symbols.
Do multiplier bombs stack or multiply?
They add, not multiply. Two 100× bombs in the same tumble = 200× total. This is an important distinction — many players assume they multiply.
Can I win from multiple clusters in one tumble?
Yes. Multiple different symbol clusters can win simultaneously. If you land 9 grapes, 8 watermelons, and 10 plums all at once, all three pay out in the same tumble.
